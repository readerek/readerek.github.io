Abstract

Research Overview

We present D3Grasp, a novel framework for dexterous grasping that addresses the challenges of grasping diverse, deformable, and articulated objects in constrained environments. Unlike existing approaches that specialize in a single object category or scenario, our method provides a unified solution applicable to a wide range of real-world objects.

D3Grasp achieves state-of-the-art performance with 96.2% success rate on diverse rigid objects, 92.7% on deformable objects, and 94.3% in constrained environments, significantly outperforming existing methods.

Our approach combines a multi-modal perception module with a hierarchical policy architecture. The perception module processes RGB-D input, tactile feedback, and proprioceptive data to estimate object properties and environmental constraints. The hierarchical policy consists of a high-level task planner and a low-level controller that adapts grasping strategies based on object category and environmental constraints.

Key innovations include a deformable object representation that predicts material behavior under manipulation, a diversity-aware grasp sampler that efficiently explores the grasp space for novel objects, and a constraint-aware motion planner that generates collision-free trajectories in confined spaces.

Objects in Evaluation

Evaluation Methodology

We conducted extensive experiments on a comprehensive dataset of 250+ objects spanning 12 categories with diverse physical properties. Our evaluation framework includes:

1. Rigid Objects: Everyday items (mugs, tools, books), industrial parts (gears, bolts), and geometrically complex objects.

2. Deformable Objects: Fabrics, plastic bags, soft toys, cables, and compressible materials.

3. Articulated Objects: Scissors, pliers, containers with movable parts.

4. Constrained Scenarios: Objects in narrow spaces, cluttered shelves, partially occluded setups.

We evaluated D3Grasp using a robotic platform with a 7-DoF robotic arm equipped with a dexterous hand and multimodal sensors (Intel RealSense D455, GelSight tactile sensors). Performance was measured across five metrics: grasp success rate, object deformation, time to successful grasp, trajectory efficiency, and robustness to sensor noise.

Grasping of Diverse Objects

Diverse Object Grasping

D3Grasp addresses the challenge of grasping geometrically diverse objects through several key innovations:

• Category-Agnostic Representation: Our approach learns a unified feature space that captures essential grasping properties regardless of object category.

• Adaptive Grasp Synthesis: We develop a grasp quality predictor that evaluates stability across different object geometries and surface properties.

• Material-Aware Contact Modeling: For each object, we predict optimal contact points and forces based on surface material properties.

D3Grasp achieved 96.2% success rate on the YCB-Video dataset, outperforming the previous state-of-the-art by 8.7%. On geometrically challenging objects, it achieved 90.3% success rate, a 15.2% improvement.

Our method demonstrates remarkable generalization capabilities, successfully grasping 92.4% of novel objects unseen during training. The system adapts grasp strategies in real-time based on object geometry, weight distribution, and surface friction properties.

Grasping of Deformable Objects

Deformable Object Manipulation

Grasping deformable objects presents unique challenges due to their non-rigid nature. D3Grasp introduces:

• Deformation Prediction Network (DPN): A physics-informed neural network that simulates object deformation under various grasp configurations.

• Adaptive Grasp Point Selection: An algorithm that selects grasp points to minimize deformation while ensuring stability.

• Compliant Control Strategy: A force-control approach that adapts to object deformation during manipulation.

On deformable objects, D3Grasp achieved 92.7% success rate, outperforming baseline methods by 18.3%. For thin plastic bags, success rate improved from 41.2% to 86.5%.

Our approach significantly reduces deformation during grasping, with an average deformation index of 0.12 compared to 0.38 for state-of-the-art methods. The system successfully handles a wide range of deformable objects including clothing, plastic packaging, soft toys, and cables.

Grasping in Constrained Environments

Constrained Environment Solutions

D3Grasp overcomes environmental constraints through innovative planning and control:

• Constraint-Aware Motion Planning: Integrates collision prediction with grasp optimization for confined spaces.

• Partial Observation Handling: Uses generative models to predict occluded object geometry.

• Hierarchical Manipulation: For cluttered environments, employs "move-and-grasp" strategies.

In constrained environments, D3Grasp achieved 94.3% success rate. In narrow spaces (clearance < 5cm), success rate improved from 36.4% to 83.7% compared to baseline methods.

Our system demonstrates remarkable efficiency in constrained scenarios, reducing average planning time by 42% compared to sampling-based methods. The constraint-aware planner generates collision-free trajectories in 96.8% of test cases, even with 50% object occlusion.
